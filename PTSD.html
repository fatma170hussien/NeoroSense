What is Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder?

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD is a mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event (e.g., war, accident, abuse, natural disaster). Symptoms persist long after the event and interfere with daily functioning. Unlike normal stress reactions, PTSD symptoms are intense, chronic, and cause significant distress.

Symptoms of PTSD

Intrusive Symptoms

  • Recurrent distressing memories or flashbacks
  • Nightmares related to the traumatic event
  • Intense emotional or physical reactions to reminders
  • Feeling as if the trauma is happening again

Avoidance

  • Avoiding places, people, or activities associated with the trauma
  • Avoiding thoughts or conversations about the event
  • Memory loss about aspects of the traumatic event
  • Emotional numbness

Negative Changes in Thinking & Mood

  • Persistent negative beliefs about self, others, or the world
  • Feelings of guilt, shame, or detachment
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Difficulty experiencing positive emotions

Changes in Arousal & Reactivity

  • Irritability, angry outbursts
  • Hypervigilance, being easily startled
  • Difficulty sleeping or concentrating
  • Self-destructive behavior

Causes & Risk Factors

Biological Factors

  • Genetics may increase vulnerability
  • Brain changes in areas controlling fear and stress response
  • Dysregulation of stress hormones
  • Differences in the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex

Psychological Factors

  • Pre-existing anxiety, depression, or personality traits
  • Ineffective coping strategies
  • Previous trauma exposure
  • Childhood adversity

Environmental Factors

  • Severity and proximity of trauma
  • Lack of social support after trauma
  • Repeated or prolonged trauma exposure
  • Life stressors after the traumatic event

Treatment Options

Psychotherapy

Trauma-focused CBT, EMDR, Exposure therapy, Cognitive processing therapy

Medications

SSRIs (sertraline, paroxetine), SNRIs, Prazosin (for nightmares)

Support & Lifestyle

Support groups, Stress management, Healthy routine, Family education

Complementary Approaches

Mindfulness, Yoga, Acupuncture, Service animals

Self-Help & When to Seek Help

Self-Help Strategies

  • Maintain a daily routine
  • Practice relaxation and mindfulness
  • Journaling to express emotions
  • Gradual exposure to safe reminders of trauma
  • Seek social support and safe relationships
  • Regular exercise and healthy nutrition
  • Limit alcohol and caffeine

When to Seek Help

  • Symptoms persist â‰¥1 month
  • Symptoms interfere with work, school, or relationships
  • Frequent flashbacks, nightmares, or panic reactions
  • Risk of self-harm, substance abuse, or severe depression
  • Difficulty functioning in daily life
  • Feeling overwhelmed or unable to cope
