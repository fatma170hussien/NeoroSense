What is Bipolar Disorder?

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a chronic mental health condition characterized by extreme mood swings that include emotional heights (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). It usually starts in late adolescence or early adulthood but can occur at any age. Unlike normal mood fluctuations, bipolar mood swings are intense, persistent, and disrupt daily life.

Difference Between Normal Mood Changes and Bipolar Disorder

Criterion Normal Mood Changes Bipolar Disorder
Trigger Mood changes linked to clear life events Mood swings occur without clear reason
Duration Short-lived, resolve in days Episodes last weeks to months
Severity Mild effect on daily activities Severe impact on work, school, and relationships
Thoughts and Behavior Generally appropriate to situation Racing thoughts, impulsive decisions, risk-taking during mania; hopelessness and suicidal thoughts during depression

Symptoms of Bipolar Disorder

Mania/Hypomania Symptoms

  • Excessive energy, decreased need for sleep
  • Talking rapidly or more than usual
  • Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity
  • Impulsivity and risky behaviors
  • Irritability or extreme euphoria
  • Racing thoughts and ideas

Depressive Episode Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness or emptiness
  • Fatigue and lack of motivation
  • Loss of interest in enjoyable activities
  • Sleep changes (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • Suicidal thoughts or feelings of worthlessness
  • Difficulty concentrating and making decisions

Causes & Risk Factors

Biological Factors

  • Genetic predisposition (family history)
  • Brain structure and neurotransmitter imbalances (dopamine, serotonin)
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Differences in brain development

Psychological Factors

  • Stressful life events
  • Co-occurring mental health conditions (anxiety, substance use)
  • Traumatic experiences
  • Personality traits

Environmental Factors

  • Childhood trauma
  • Lack of social support
  • Substance abuse
  • Major life changes or stressors

Diagnosis & Assessment

Diagnostic Process

  • Clinical interviews and history taking
  • Physical examination to rule out medical causes
  • Psychiatric evaluation
  • Input from family members

Screening Tools

  • Young Mania Rating Scale (YMRS)
  • Mood Disorder Questionnaire (MDQ)
  • Bipolar Spectrum Diagnostic Scale
  • Clinical interviews based on DSM-5 criteria

DSM-5 Criteria

  • Presence of at least one manic episode (with or without depression)
  • Symptoms not attributable to substance use or medical condition
  • Significant impairment in social or occupational functioning
  • Rule out other psychiatric disorders

Treatment Options

Medications

Mood stabilizers (Lithium, Valproate), Antipsychotics (Olanzapine, Quetiapine), Antidepressants (with caution, usually combined with stabilizers)

Psychotherapy

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Interpersonal and Social Rhythm Therapy, Family-focused therapy, Psychoeducation

Lifestyle Changes

Regular sleep and exercise, Stress management techniques, Avoiding alcohol and drugs, Maintaining a consistent routine

Other Treatments

Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT) for severe cases, Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS), Support groups and peer support

Self-Help & When to Seek Help

Self-Help Strategies

  • Keeping a mood diary
  • Following a consistent daily routine
  • Practicing relaxation techniques (meditation, breathing)
  • Building strong support networks
  • Recognizing early warning signs of relapse
  • Maintaining a healthy lifestyle
  • Educating yourself about the condition
  • Setting realistic goals and expectations

When to Seek Professional Help

  • If mood swings last for weeks or interfere with school/work
  • If risky or impulsive behaviors appear
  • If depressive symptoms lead to suicidal thoughts
  • If daily functioning is significantly impaired
  • If relationships are suffering due to mood symptoms
  • If you're having difficulty managing symptoms on your own
  • If substance use becomes a coping mechanism

Need Immediate Help?

If you or someone you know is experiencing a mental health crisis, don't hesitate to seek help immediately
